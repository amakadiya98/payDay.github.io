Payday Loans Online

Unexpected financial emergencies can be unsettling, but they don’t have to ruin your week. Thanks to the quick and easy funding of payday loans, you may even be able to solve your financial crisis on your lunch break. Payday loans are helpful solutions for borrowers with less-than-perfect credit or those who don’t want to jump through the hoops of a traditional bank loan, but before you get a payday loan, you should read on for an in-depth review of how these loans work and whether they are right for your situation.

What are
Payday Loans?
Payday loans are small principal, high-interest loans that are typically due to be repaid within a few weeks. These loans are structured for people who need quick, easy cash to hold them over “until payday”. Payday lenders make it easy to get approved and receive your funds, but they typically charge high interest rates for the convenience and lower credit standards.

How do Payday Loans work?

  1. Once you are approved for a payday loan, your lender will ask you to complete some basic paperwork that can usually be completed online from the convenience of your home.
  2. You’ll also need to share your bank account information so you can receive your funds.
  3. our loan repayment terms will vary by lender and loan amount, but some payday lenders will expect the entire balance of the loan with interest repaid within 2-4 weeks.

For example, if you borrowed $300, your lender might set up an auto draft from your checking account to repay $340 two weeks later (the original principal plus the interest and fees). For larger loan amounts, your lender may arrange automatic payments every two weeks until the loan is repaid. In some cases, you may be able to mail in your payments, but these arrangements are the exception, not the standard.

Fees to Get Loans from Payday Loan Online Lenders

When shopping for payday loans, you may see several types of fees that are applied on top of the principal loan amount. Expect to pay a small application fee, especially if your lender requires a background check. Most lenders also charge origination fees, which can be a flat fee or a percentage of the requested loan amount, to cover the administration costs of processing and maintaining your loan.

Some lenders also have fees that are avoidable and only apply in certain situations. For example, if you miss a loan payment, you may be charged a late fee. Some lenders also charge pre-payment fees if you pay your loan off early, while others may charge renewal fees if you decide to renew, or take your loan out again, at the end of a payment cycle. These fees add to your overall cost of borrowing money and should be avoided whenever possible, so make sure you have a plan for repaying the loan and sticking to the repayment terms as closely as possible.

Steps to take before applying for Payday Loans

Payday loans are popular because they generally are simple to get and provide fast funding. Unfortunately, this ease of access has caused some negative stigma around payday loans, especially when borrowers don’t do proper research first. To make sure this type of loan is a good fit, you should always do the following before applying:

1. Choose the right type of loan

Although instant payday loans are a very specific type of financial product, some companies offer products called “payday loans” that don’t truly fit the definition. For example, some companies may ask you to provide collateral to secure the loan, which puts your personal property at risk. Know the specifics of your loan before applying.

2. Consider your other options

Payday loans aren’t a good fit for every borrower. If you need a short-term influx of cash to solve a financial crunch, you might also consider options like getting a second job, using credit cards, or borrowing from a friend or family member.

3. Check the lender’s requirements

Each lender has different requirements for proof of income, credit scores, loan amounts, and repayment terms. Before applying with a lender, be sure its requirements can meet your loan needs.

4. Understand your state laws

Each state’s Attorney General has the authority to place limitations and regulations on payday lending or outlaw it entirely. These laws work to prevent predatory lending practices that take advantage of low-income families, and payday lenders must abide by them. Educate yourself on these laws and verify that your lender complies with any applicable state statutes.

Basic Requirements for Getting an Online Loan

Instant payday loans are regulated at the state level, so lenders must follow the guidelines of the states in which they operate. For this reason, requirements to get a payday loan will vary from lender to lender and state to state. In general, however, lenders require several basic things to qualify for a loan:

Age

In order to be legally bound by the payday loan contract, the borrower must be at least 18 years of age.

Active checking account (Credit Score)

Many payday lenders don’t deal in cash or issue checks for your initial loan proceeds – they will want to direct deposit your funds into your active checking account. Many lenders also require your loan repayments to be auto drafted from the same account.

Proof of income

Income requirements will vary by location, but lenders want to see documents proof that you can repay the loan when it’s due. While this doesn’t need to be income from a traditional job, it does need to be recurring, consistent income.

Address, phone number and an email

If you fail to make your payments on time, your lender will want to know how to contact you. You’ll need to provide a stable home address, contact phone number, and email address. Many lenders will also ask for contact information for references as well.

Reasons why you might have been rejected

Although the requirements on payday loans are more relaxed that with other loans, lenders still deny loan applications if they feel the risk of default on the loan is too high. If your application is declined, it may be because:

You are not employed or your income is too low

If your income isn’t enough to cover basic living expenses and make your loan payments, lenders may deny your application for fear that they won’t recoup the loan.

You already have a payday loan or recently borrowed one

Each state has different limitations, but most states limit borrowers to a single payday loan outstanding. In states where multiple payday loans are allowed, borrowers are typically limited to $500 – $1,000 in loans at any given time.

You have too many current debts

Since lenders don’t look heavily at your credit scores, they will likely look at your current debts to gauge your ability to repay.
