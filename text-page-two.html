Payday loans are a quick cash alternative that let consumers borrow against their paycheck. They’re also easier to obtain than other types of unsecured loans for people with lower credit scores. How do Payday Loans work?

Payday loans are essentially an advance on your next paycheck. They’re a type of short-term loan with usually a 14-day repayment term in a lump sum. They have many other names, including:

  • Paycheck advance
  • Cash advance
  • Bad credit loan
  • Deferred deposit transaction

According to the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau (CFPB), the typical payday loan is for $350, but they can range from $100 to $1,000 or more.

Once approved for the loan, you write a post-dated check for the loan amount plus fees or allow the lender to electronically debit a bank, prepaid card, or credit union account. The company then gives you money in cash or deposits the funds into your account.

At the agreed date, the company deposits your check or debits your account. Some states allow you to roll over your loan if you don’t have the money when it’s due. The lender then charges you an additional fee and extends your financing for another term. Many states set limits on the number of times you can do this.

Pros and Cons of Payday Loans

Pros

  • Payday loans provide quick access to money with only a few requirements.
  • There are no limitations on how you can use your payday loan money.
  • With some lenders, you can have the money in your account the same day if you apply early enough. If not, the next business day is typical.
  • You can complete online payday loan applications in just a few minutes from the comfort of your home and without having to talk to lenders.
  • Payday loans are unsecured, so you don’t have to provide personal property as collateral.
  • Most payday loan lenders don’t run credit checks, which means there’s no impact on your credit score from a hard credit inquiry and you can still be approved if you don’t have any credit history.

Cons

  • Payday loans have excessive APRs that are 10 or more times higher than high-interest credit cards.
  • Payday loans are tied to your bank account for repayment and can cause multiple overdraft charges if your account is underfunded on the day the loan is due.
  • The CFPB reports that 85% of borrowers renew or default on their loans, perpetuating a cycle of debt that’s difficult to leave.

Requirements for Getting an Online Loan

Payday loans are easier to get than many other types of loans, even if you have bad credit. The CFPB lists the following as the basic requirements:

  • At least 18 years old.
  • Proof of stable income from a job or other sources.
  • Valid identification, such as a state-issued I.D
  • Active bank, prepaid card, or credit union account.

A few lenders may also check your credit score or request additional personal documents or details.

Payday Loans Are An Option

Payday loans get a bad rap, but in many states, they are a legal and viable option for people with poor to no credit who require immediate cash for an emergency.
